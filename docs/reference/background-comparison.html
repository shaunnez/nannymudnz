Background Analysis

LF2 structure vs what we built — and how to fix it

Reference: LF2 Fan Stage

✓ LF2 — continuous filled backdrop
  • Blue stone wall fills every pixel of the backdrop — no empty space
  • Pillars, arch, torches are baked into the wall scene — not floating sprites
  • Clear ground plane (reflective blue floor) meets wall at a sharp horizon line
  • Torch flames are the only separate animated elements

Current: Our Night Market

⚠ Ours — isolated props with empty gaps
  • Individual props (lanterns, stalls) every 280px with large empty dark gaps
  • Midground is just a gradient — nothing fills the wall area behind the props
  • Feels like props placed on an empty stage, not being somewhere
  • No horizon strip between ground and backdrop

How LF2 Layers Work

SKY
LAYER
~60% of screen height
Wide painted bitmap that tiles horizontally. Sky gradient, clouds, distant scenery (mountains, city silhouette). Scrolls at 0.1–0.2× camera. Covers every pixel — no gaps.
WALL
BACKDROP
~30–40% of screen height above ground
The main scene — castle wall, market shopfronts, tree line, cave ceiling. A tileable image (400–600px wide) that repeats across the full world width. Scrolls at 0.3–0.5×.
HORIZON
STRIP
~20–30px tall band
Thin decorative strip where backdrop meets ground. Stone ledge, wooden planks, grass edge, wall base. Tiled. Creates the visual "seam" between floor and background scene.
GROUND
PLANE
~40% of screen height
Flat solid-colour plane — dirt, flagstone, grass. Simple gradient. Perspective lines (diagonal). Characters walk on this. Already fine in our game.

Our Current Assembly Hall

Assembly — better than Market but same issue

The rug and floor look great. The pillars and banners have the right idea but are too sparse — the wall between them is just a plain colour gradient. In LF2 the wall behind pillars would be a continuous brick/stone texture.

The Fix

Replace scattered props → tiled backdrop images

  • Generate one tileable ~400px wide image per stage using PixelLab — e.g. "night market shopfront wall with lanterns, pixel art side view, seamlessly tileable"
  • Use Phaser TileSprite to repeat this image across the full 4000px world width at the horizon band
  • Add a thin 20px horizon strip tile (stone ledge / wooden plank edge) where backdrop meets ground
  • Keep individual prop sprites only for rare feature pieces (e.g. the throne, the main bell post) — not repeated every 280px
  • Sky and ground procedural drawing stays as-is — it already looks good
